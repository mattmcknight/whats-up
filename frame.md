# What's Up - Frame

## Source

> I am always sitting here working, missing the meeting I am supposed to be in. The notifications aren't working. I need something to actually try to start the zoom meeting at the time the meeting starts. Some kind of red bar at the top of the screen that shows I am supposed to be in a meeting?
>
> Also, maybe it can integrate with Todoist so I can keep track of which task I am currently working on. (kind of like the task order up idea The Task Order Up! - DSri Seah, combined with the emergent task planner, combined with the current meaning)

> I usually don't work full screen, but sound would be good - something persistent, flashing after the start time, alarm-style, changing the task bar color, something very very noticeable for when I am absorbed in a task.

> Todoist integration is sort of phase two, maybe just a basic - this is what I am working on kind of thing.

> Calendar stuff is in both Microsoft 365 and Gmail, but both are in my Apple Calendar on the desktop, which just consistently fails to notify me.

> It needs to be a little bit different for all the meetings I can take in person. I work in the office, but most people are remote.

---

## Problem

macOS Calendar notifications fail silently or get dismissed mid-focus. Meetings start without awareness. Zoom meetings go unjoined. There is no persistent, aggressive indicator of "you should be in a meeting right now." The 15-minute-early default notification fires while still in a prior meeting and is useless.

Secondary: no ambient awareness of what task is currently in progress.

## Outcome

A persistent, impossible-to-ignore system that:
1. Shows what meeting is coming next at all times
2. Aggressively alarms when a meeting starts - sound, flashing, color - and won't stop until acknowledged
3. Auto-launches Zoom for remote meetings, just needs acknowledgment for in-person ones
4. Optionally shows what task is currently being worked on
